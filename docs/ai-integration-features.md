Natural-language capture — type or speak something like "spent 24 on lunch" or "remind me to call the dentist tomorrow at 3" and it figures out whether it's an expense, income, todo, reminder, note, meeting, or budget, extracts the details, and pre-fills the entry.

Confirmation before writing — every AI-created entry shows an editable card (amount, category, date, etc.) that the user confirms before it's saved. Nothing gets written silently.

Conversational query — answer questions about the user's own data: "how much did I spend on food this month?", "what's on my plate today?", "did I log rent?", "what's my budget left for dining?"

Daily briefing — a summary card (on Home) of today's meetings, due todos, reminders, budget status, and anything overdue. Optional evening recap.

Proactive insights / nudges — alerts like "you're at 90% of your dining budget with 8 days left," "3 tasks are overdue," or "you broke your pomodoro streak." Delivered as cards and optional notifications.

Cross-module reasoning — connections across features, e.g. "you have a dinner meeting Friday — want to set a budget for it?"

Unified intelligent search — one place to ask across expenses, income, todos, notes, meetings, and reminders at once.

Conversation memory — remembers context within a chat so follow-ups work ("…and how about last month?").
Smart suggestions / autofill — suggests category, account, or recurrence based on past entries (e.g. "lunch" usually → Food category).
Recurring detection — notices repeated entries (rent, subscriptions) and offers to make them recurring.
Spending/activity summaries on demand — "summarize my week" or "where did my money go this month" with a quick breakdown.
Voice input — speak entries and queries instead of typing.
Graceful offline/fallback behavior — simple captures (expenses, reminders) still work with no network via a lightweight on-device parser; richer queries degrade to manual entry rather than failing.
